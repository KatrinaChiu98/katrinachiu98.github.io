Project case study

Project Management: Inter-App Data Flow

Role: Project Coordinator / Analyst

Summary: Coordinated stakeholders and timelines across multiple systems to deliver a working inter-application data flow, improving visibility and reducing manual effort.

Context

Legacy processes relied on manual data transfers between systems, causing delays, errors, and a lack of end-to-end visibility.

Contribution

Led requirements clarification, coordinated technical teams, and tracked risks and dependencies across the delivery timeline.

Impact

Reduced manual reconciliation work and improved data consistency across applications, supporting better reporting and decision-making.

1. Project background

Briefly describe the systems involved, why the integration was needed, and what success looked like from a business perspective (e.g. faster processing, fewer errors, better reporting).

2. Stakeholders & requirements

  • Identified key stakeholders across operations, IT, and vendor teams.
  • Captured functional requirements (what data needed to move where and when).
  • Documented assumptions, constraints, and non-functional needs (SLAs, monitoring, audit).

3. Planning & delivery

  • Created a delivery plan with milestones for design, build, testing, and go-live.
  • Coordinated hand-offs between internal teams and external vendors.
  • Tracked risks and issues, escalating where timelines or dependencies were at risk.

4. Results & outcomes

Summarise the outcome: what changed for users, what metrics improved, and any qualitative feedback from stakeholders or end-users.

5. Lessons learned

Reflect on what worked well (e.g. communication cadence, documentation) and what you would adjust in future projects (e.g. earlier involvement of certain teams, clearer acceptance criteria).
