Project case study

Requirements Analysis: Integration Requirements Report

Role: Business Analyst

Summary: Led the discovery and documentation of integration requirements for a new solution, aligning technical design with business needs and constraints.

Objective

Define clear, testable requirements for integrating multiple systems as part of a wider change initiative.

Scope

Covered data flows, business rules, error handling, and reporting needs across the integration.

Outcome

Produced a structured requirements report used by technical teams for design and build, reducing ambiguity and rework.

1. Context & objectives

Describe the business problem, the systems involved, and what the organisation wanted to achieve with the integration (e.g. single customer view, automated processing, improved reporting).

2. Information gathering

  • Held workshops and interviews with key stakeholders and subject-matter experts.
  • Reviewed existing documentation, process maps, and reporting requirements.
  • Identified pain points in the current process and opportunities to streamline flows.

3. Requirements documentation

  • Captured functional requirements for data exchange, validation, and transformation.
  • Documented non-functional requirements (performance, availability, security, audit).
  • Used clear numbering and structure so developers and testers could reference requirements easily.

4. Prioritisation & sign-off

Explain how you prioritised requirements (e.g. MoSCoW, risk/impact), managed trade-offs, and secured sign-off from stakeholders and sponsors.

5. Reflection & next steps

Reflect on how the requirements pack supported later phases (design, build, testing), and what you would refine in future work (templates, stakeholder engagement, traceability).
